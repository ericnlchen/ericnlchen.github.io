Responsive Redesign—Fishing in Japan

by Eric Chen

Choosing a Website to Redesign

I love fishing, but many fishing websites have outdated or inaccessible designs. The website of a local bait & tackle shop, Lucky Bait and Tackle in Warren, RI has a number of usability limitations, and I'd like to try improving it to make it more accessible to all fisherfolk! Finding Problems

The first step in the redesign is identifying all of the current problems with the website. Here are some of the major ones I discovered as I tried to use it:

Efficiency Problems:

  • Inconsistent font sizes and styles makes it slow to identify key information
  • Embedding a bunch of YouTube videos in the homepage is distracting and makes me forget what I was doing
  • Animated "advertisements" like the "Reel Repair" one aren't clickable, so even if I want to repair my reel it takes me a while to figure out where I should navigate to do that
Learnability Problems:
  • With a variety of images, animations, and text-based links, its hard to know what is actually clickable
  • Poor use of Gestalt principles for visual grouping (especially the lack of alignment in the leftmost column)
Memorability Problems:
  • Nonstandard colors of already-clicked links makes it hard to remember where you've already visited

In addition to these problems, WAVE detected 16 accessibility errors and 42 low contrast errors. 15 of the 16 accessibility issues were for images missing alt text. While many of the low contrast errors were not identifiable to me, the main title with the name of the store did seem low contrast (orange on blue).
